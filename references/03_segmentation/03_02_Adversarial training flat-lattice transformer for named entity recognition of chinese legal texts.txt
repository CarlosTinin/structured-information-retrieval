Abstract
Judgment documents are the legally binding written conclusion made by the court
based on the facts of the case and the law. Due to the use of professional terms and
nested combinations of words, potential information of judgment documents has
not been deeply excavated. Named Entity Recognition (NER) is a necessary task in
Natural Language Processing (NLP), and has been widely introduced into Chinese
texts processing for many years. However, the professional terms and nested words
lead to the boundaries between entities being blurred, which can not accurately
divide entities. In this paper, a new NER model Adversarial Training Flat-Lattice
Transformer (AT-Flat) which combines adversarial training and Flat-Lattice Transformer (Flat) is proposed to weaken these problems. In AT-Flat, the Flat combines
character and word information to get sequence information, and the CRF is used
to output the final entity prediction results. Moreover, the key point is an adversarial
training framework introduced to integrate task-shared word boundary information from Chinese Word Segmentation (CWS) task into Chinese NER task. The
framework is able to filter out the noise caused by CWS task and further enhance
the effect of Chinese NER task. More importantly, experiments on NER task of
Chinese traffic accident and financial lending judgment documents show that our
method outperforms other state-of-the-art methods. These verify that our method
can effectively alleviate the problem of poor NER effect caused by professional
terms and nested words. In addition, three public Chinese NER datasets were also
used to evaluate our method.

1 Introduction
The amount of data in the judicial field has increased rapidly in recent years. Legal
professionals such as judges, lawyers, and prosecutors not only handle a large number of cases, but also need to consult numerous references. Judgment documents are
the legally binding written conclusion made by the court based on the facts of the
case and the law. With the development of judicial online system, nearly 100 million
judgment documents have been published online in China, accumulating abundant
judicial data of various cases (Tang et al. 2020). Judgment documents can help professionals get similar cases to assist in sentencing. The information mining of judgment documents can reduce the labor cost of professionals and improve legal work
efficiency.
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a common way for processing data, of
which Named Entity Recognition (NER) is an indispensable research topic. NER
aims to extract the words or expressions denoting specific entities from documents
(Huang et al. 2020), which is composed of two parts: detecting the entity boundaries
and classifying the entities into predefined categories such as Name, Judicial Organization, and Location (Tang et al. 2020). Chinese judicial NER can improve the
efficiency of court operations and provide support to judges in their trial work.
Due to the differences from other languages such as English, there are significant
challenges in the Chinese NER task. The reasons are as follows: first, the Chinese
language doesn’t have spaces between words and lacks semantic features like uppercase, which makes many NER methods are not suitable for Chinese; secondly, the
Chinese dataset for NLP is small compared to the English dataset; third, there are
multiple meanings of words in Chinese, and the meaning of the same word may be
vary in different contexts (Chang et al. 2021).
Furthermore, judicial entities are mostly nested, making structures complicated.
For example, the nested types of the legal entities in Fig. 1, include "Judicial Organization and Location", "Causing-trouble Vehicle and defendant", "Location and Defendant", and “Stolen Goods and Causing-trouble Vehicle". "
(Chengdu Intermediate People’s Procuratorate)" is supposed to be categorized into
’Judicial Organization’, but "
(Chengdu)" may be categorized into ’Location’
in other text (Tang et al. 2020). On the one hand, it leads to a decline in the accuracy of entity recognition. On the other hand, it loses the correlation between words,
which also affects the subsequent downstream tasks (Zhang et al. 2023). These entities are rare in other domains, which makes some existing NER methods ineffective
for the judicial documents.
Although existing NER methods achieve great success in many domains, most of
them normally ignore the nested nature of named entities (Wang et al. 2022). There
are some researches on the nested NER task by applying deep learning models in
resent years, such as Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), attention mechanism, and
neural layered model. Ju et al. (2018) proposed a novel neural model to identify
nested entities by dynamically stacking flat NER layers. Each flat NER layer is based
on a bidirectional long short-term memory (BiLSTM) layer to capture sequential
context representation and a Conditional Random Field (CRF) decoder. Fei et al.
(2020) introduced a dispatched attention neural model with multi-task learning for

Wang et al. (2020) presented Pyramid, a novel layered model for nested NER. In the

There are probably only few methods are effective in the Chinese judicial NER
task for the above problems. As to the techniques applied in NER, there are three
streams (Li et al. 2020): 1) Rule-based approaches, which do not need annotated data
as they rely on hand-crafted rules; 2) Statistic-based methods, including conditional
random field (Chen et al. 2006), maximum entropy model (Bender et al. 2003), and
hidden Markov model (Morwal et al. 2012), etc. 3) Deep-learning based approaches.
Rule-based and statistic-based methods are very corpus-dependent and the experts
need to manually extract features from the dataset. Due to domain-specific rules
and incomplete dictionaries, high precision and low recall are often observed from
these methods, and they cannot be transferred to other domains (Li et al. 2020). In
recent years, with the development of deep learning, deep-learning NER models have
received more attention and achieved better results. However, deep-learning methods
predict each character with a given feature independently without considering the
previously predicted labels, which may lead to invalid predicted label sequences and
thus reduce the accuracy of entity recognition results (Tang et al. 2020). These NER
methods mentioned above are rarely applied to the judicial field.

2 Related work
The processing methods for Chinese legal texts are mainly applied to judgment prediction, case text classification and information extraction. Liu and Hsieh (2006)
built a domain dependent word list with the help of Chien’s PAT tree-based method
and HowNet, and used the resulting word list for defining relevant phrases for classifying Chinese judicial documents. Xiao et al. (2018) implemented several conventional text classification baselines for judgment prediction and experimental results
have showed that it is still a challenge for current models to predict the judgment
results of legal cases, especially on prison terms. Chen et al. (2020) proposed a Legal
Triplet Extraction System for drug-related criminal judgment documents. The system
extracts the entities and the semantic relations jointly and benefits from the proposed
legal lexicon feature and multi-task learning framework. Wang et al. (2020) divided
entity type into four categories, and presented a model of NER based on attention
mechanism; the structure improvement of input vector fusion CNN embed mode.
In the judicial field, only a few researchs on named entity recognition for Chinese legal texts. Shi et al. (2022) established the corpus dataset of specific cause of
action and proposed RoBERTa (a variant of the pre-trained language model BERT)BiLSTM-CRF legal case entity recognition model to improve the performance of
Chinese legal NER. Zhang et al. (2023) respectively used RoBERTa and Skip-Gram
method to extract char-level and word-level feature of a legal document, and fused
them. Dong et al. (2022) developed NERP, NERCGP, and NERFPP to expand the
named entity recognition corpus in the judicial field, and introduced BERT-WWM
(whole-word mask)-EXT(extra) and BiLSTM-CRF for Chinese NER task. Lee et al.

research on the processing of judgment documents by NER technology is still in its
infancy (Yuan and Zhang 2021).

3 Methodology
Though various NER methods have been proposed in recent years, most of the existing methods only considered one kind of word-enhanced information, which may
not be effective for the Chinese characters. In addition, there are rare NER methods
applied to Chinese judgment documents, so it is necessary for us to propose a model
that can be well applied in this field.
Aiming at the problem of nested entities in Chinese legal texts, we proposed a
method that combines adversarial training and add word information into the NER
task. It mainly includes two parts: (1) Combining character and word information to
make each Chinese character provide more valuable resources for the model than a
single word or character, and provide more help for NER task. (2) The adversarial
training framework extractes the common features of CWS and NER task, while the
noise of CWS task can be filtered. It makes the boundary of legal entities more clear
and improve the effect of legal nested NER.
3.1 Model architecture
Generative Adversarial Net (GAN) (Goodfellow et al. 2020) have received considerable attention since the 2014 groundbreaking work (Wang 2020). GAN is inspired by
a minimax two-player game. The optimization goal is to achieve Nash equilibrium
so that the generator can estimate the distribution of data samples (Su et al. 2022).
GAN is mainly used in computer vision (Mirza and Osindero 2014). In recent years,
the idea of adversarial training is introduced in NLP. Different from computer vision,
the input sequences in NLP are discrete values. Although the adversarial training has
been successfully applied in some fields of NLP, there is little research in the judicial
domain to use the idea of adversarial training to the NER task of Chinese judgment
documents. In addition, the Chinese NER task and CWS task have many similar
word boundaries. Most existing NER methods do not fully exploit the word boundary
information of CWS.
In this paper, a novel word-enhanced model called AT-Flat is proposed that combines adversarial training and add word information into the NER task. The first
dynamic framework is the adversarial training which aims to extract common features of the Chinese CWS task and NER task. The another dynamic framework is
the Flat-Lattice Transformer for the NER task. With the power of Transformer and
well-designed position encoding, FLAT can fully leverage the sequence information
between words.

3.2 Embeddings
The first step is character embedding, which aims to map discrete characters into
the distribute representations. The BIO annotation method is used to label data in
character form, which B represents the beginning part of the entity; I represents the
middle part of the entity, and O represents the non-entity. For example, to label a
person entity, the first character is labeled B-PER, and the other characters are labeled
I-PER. For a given Chinese sentence x = {c1 , c2 , . . . , cn } from Chinese NER dataset or CWS dataset, we lookup embedding vector from pre-trained embedding matrix
for each character ci as xi ∈ Rde . The word embedding will be introduced in the

3.4 Flat-lattice transformer
Focusing on the NER task, the flat-lattice architecture (Li et al. 2020) uses Transformer encoder. It is mainly composed of self-attention and feedforward network
(FFN) layers. Each sublayer is followed by residual connection and layer normalization. FFN is a position-wise multi-layer perceptron with non-linear transformation.
The flat-lattice is defined as a set of spans, and a span corresponds to a token, a
head and a tail, like in Fig. 4. The token is a character or word. The head and tail
represent the position index of the token’s first and last characters in a sequence, and
they indicate the position of the token in the lattice. The head and tail are the same
for each character.
The flat-lattice structure consists of spans with different lengths. The relative position encoding of spans is used to encode the interactions among spans. For two spans
xi and xj in the lattice, according to their heads and tails, there are three kinds of
relations between them: intersection, inclusion and separation. A dense vector is used
to represent the relationships, and it is calculated by continuous transformations of
the head and tail information. So it can not only represent the relation between two
tokens, but also show more detailed information, such as the distance between words.

5 Conclusion
In this paper, we propose AT-Flat, an adversarial training model integrates task-shared
word boundary information from CWS to improve Chinese judgment documents
NER performance, and combines character and word information to get more legal
entities information. Extensive experiments based on two different Chinese judgment
document datasets and three public Chinese NER datasets show that AT-Flat achieves
the best performance compared to the state-of-the-art models. For future work, We
will adjust the model to expand its application fields.
Although AT-Flat achieves strong performance, it still has some limitations. One
potential bottleneck is its limited capacity to model long-range dependencies, which
may affect its effectiveness when processing lengthy legal documents. Additionally,
the model’s inference efficiency might degrade as input length increases, posing chal-
